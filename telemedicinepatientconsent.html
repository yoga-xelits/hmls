7.RISKS, CONSEQUENCES & BENEFITS

Expected Benefits:

  1. Improved access to rehabilitation care by enabling a patient to stay in his or her home (remote site) while the rehabilitation therapist evaluates, treats and educates the patient and his or her family members through telehealth services.
  2. More efficient rehabilitation evaluation and treatment.
  3. Better insight of home environment through video conference.
  4. Obtaining expertise of a distant specialist.

Potential Risks:

As with any medical practice, there are potential risks associated with Telehealth practice. These risks include but are not limited to –

  1. In rare cases, information transmitted might not be of poor resolution, to allow for appropriate decision making by the rehabilitation therapist.
  2. Rehabilitation therapists lack the ability to use their palpation skills due to remote video conferencing and hence must rely on the patient feedback.
  3. Delays in medical evaluation and treatment can occur due to deficiencies or failure of technology/equipment.
  4. In very rare instances, security protocols may fail, causing a breach of privacy of personal medical information.
  5. In rare cases, lack of access to complete medical information can lead to judgement errors.
